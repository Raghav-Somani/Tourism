Description

The Chittor Fort or Chittorgarh is the largest fort in India. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The fort was the capital of Mewar and is located in the present-day town of Chittor. It sprawls over a hill 180 m (590.6 ft) in height spread over an area of 280 ha (691.9 acres) above the plains of the valley drained by the Berach River. The fort precinct has several historical palaces, gates, temples and two prominent commemorative towers.

What Can You Do ?

  • Wonder at the massive structure of the fort.
  • Walk around the ruins and get a feel about what life used to be like in the past.
  • Offer your Prayers at the temples in the premises.
  • Take tour of The Palace Of Padmini
  • Click multiple photographs of this world heritage site.

Availability Of Guide ?

Professional guides speaking English and Hindi are available by the ticket counter for a nominal fee of 500 INR. You can hire a guide as per your choice to get a better understanding of the majestic fort.
The Nearest Airport is Tirupati Airport.
